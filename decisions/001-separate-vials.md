# Decision 001: Separate Vials Over KLOW Blend

**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Final
**Impact:** Core thesis of the project

## Context

The company sells a "KLOW Blend" (GHK-Cu 50mg / BPC-157 10mg / TB-500 10mg / KPV 10mg) as a single lyophilized vial for $458/kit.

## Decision

Recommend purchasing the same peptides as three separate products instead of the KLOW blend.

## Rationale

1. **Chemistry:** Cu²⁺ in GHK-Cu catalyzes methionine oxidation in BPC-157 and TB-500, producing methionine sulfoxide variants with altered/unknown bioactivity
2. **Price:** Separate products cost $456 — $2 cheaper
3. **Flexibility:** Subjects can dose each compound independently and adjust as needed
4. **Stability:** Each compound is stored in its chemically appropriate environment
5. **The company's own product line validates this** — they sell BPC/TB4 and GHK-Cu/KPV as separate blends that are chemically sound

## Alternatives Considered

- **KLOW Blend as-is:** Rejected due to degradation chemistry
- **Higher KLOW doses to compensate for degradation:** Rejected — this is guessing, creates unknown byproducts, and is an implicit admission of instability
- **Custom compounding:** Not available from this vendor

## Consequences

- Subjects need 3 separate vials and 3 separate syringes per day instead of 1
- Slightly more complex daily routine
- Need separate reconstitution for each
- These are acceptable tradeoffs for chemical integrity
