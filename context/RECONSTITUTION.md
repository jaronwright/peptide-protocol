# Reconstitution & Injection Guide

## Equipment Required

- Bacteriostatic water (BAC) — 30ml vials
- U-100 insulin syringes — 1ml, 29–31 gauge, ½ inch
- Alcohol swabs
- Sharps container

## Reconstitution Volumes

| Peptide | Vial Size | BAC Water | Concentration | Dose | Draw |
|---------|-----------|-----------|---------------|------|------|
| BPC-157/TB-500 | 5mg/5mg | 1ml (100 units) | 5mg/ml each | 500mcg | 10 units (0.1ml) |
| KPV | 10mg | 2ml (200 units) | 5mg/ml | 500mcg | 10 units (0.1ml) |
| GHK-Cu | 50mg | 5ml (500 units) | 10mg/ml | 1mg or 2mg | 10 or 20 units |

## How to Reconstitute (Mix a New Vial)

1. **Clean:** Wipe the rubber vial stopper with an alcohol swab. Let air dry ~5 seconds.

2. **Draw BAC water:** Using a fresh syringe, slowly draw the correct volume of bacteriostatic water. Pull plunger steadily to minimize air bubbles.

3. **Inject into vial:** Insert needle through the stopper at a slight angle. Release water **slowly down the inside glass wall**. NEVER spray directly onto the lyophilized powder — this can damage peptide structure through shear force.

4. **Dissolve:** Wait 2–3 minutes. Gently swirl the vial — NEVER shake. Solution should be completely clear with no visible particles or cloudiness.

5. **Label:** Write today's date on the vial with a marker.

6. **Store:** Upright in refrigerator at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Good for exactly **28 days** from reconstitution date. Discard after 28 days regardless of remaining volume.

## How to Inject (Daily)

Repeat this process for each of the three peptides. **Always use a fresh syringe for each compound.**

1. **Draw dose:** Alcohol-swab the vial top. Insert syringe, flip vial upside down, slowly pull plunger to the correct unit mark.

2. **Clear air:** Hold syringe needle-up. Flick the barrel gently — bubbles rise to top. Push plunger slightly until a tiny drop appears at the needle tip.

3. **Select site:** Pinch a fold of skin. Standard sites:
   - Abdomen (2 inches from navel) — most common
   - Upper thigh — alternate
   - Upper arm — alternate
   - Near injury site (Subject B: right knee, lower back) — for BPC/TB4 only
   - **Rotate sites daily** to prevent irritation

4. **Inject:** Insert needle at 45° angle into the pinched skin fold. Push plunger slowly and steadily. Withdraw needle, apply light pressure with a clean alcohol swab.

## Injection Route

All peptides in this protocol are administered **subcutaneously (SubQ)** — into the fat layer under the skin. This is NOT intramuscular.

## Timing

- All three injections can be administered at the same time
- Morning preferred (empty stomach)
- Can inject before or after workouts — at least 30 minutes spacing from training is ideal
- Wait 20 minutes after injection before eating

## Critical Rules

1. **One syringe = one compound.** NEVER draw multiple peptides into the same syringe.
2. **Never reuse syringes.** Dispose in sharps container immediately after use.
3. **28-day rule:** Throw away any reconstituted vial older than 28 days.
4. **Visual inspection:** If solution is cloudy, has particles, or changed color → discard immediately.
5. **Temperature:** Keep reconstituted vials refrigerated at all times. Never freeze reconstituted peptides. Unreconstituted (lyophilized) vials can be stored at room temperature or refrigerated.
6. **Light:** Avoid prolonged light exposure. Store in original packaging or a dark area of the fridge.
