# Test Subject Profiles

## Subject A

- **Age:** 24
- **Sex:** Female
- **Height:** 5'8"
- **Weight:** 120 lbs
- **Activities:** Cycling, weight lifting, swimming

### Protocol
- BPC-157/TB-500: 500mcg/day, 5 vials, cycles
- KPV: 500mcg/day, 5 vials, continuous
- GHK-Cu: 1mg/day, 2 vials, cycles

### GHK-Cu Rationale
At 24, natural GHK-Cu levels are still near peak. 1mg/day is a conservative supplemental dose appropriate for her age. Only 2 vials because 2 × 28 days = 56 days = exactly one 8-week ON cycle, which is sufficient for a younger subject.

### Known Waste
GHK-Cu: 22mg per vial × 2 vials = 44mg total waste (unavoidable at 1mg/day due to 28-day shelf life vs 50-day vial supply)

### BAC Water
25ml total → 1 × 30ml vial

## Subject B

- **Age:** 33
- **Sex:** Male
- **Height:** 5'9"
- **Weight:** 160 lbs
- **Activities:** Cycling (road, high-end setup), weight lifting, swimming

### Medical History (Relevant to Protocol)
- Right knee: partial meniscus removal (childhood wrestling injury → torn dancing → retore → surgery for meniscus stuck in kneecap)
- QL/lower back issues developed post-surgery
- Weekly chiropractor visits
- Chronically tight calves

### Protocol
- BPC-157/TB-500: 500mcg/day, 5 vials, cycles
- KPV: 500mcg/day, 5 vials, continuous
- GHK-Cu: 2mg/day, 8 vials, cycles

### GHK-Cu Rationale
At 33, natural GHK-Cu levels have declined ~25% from peak. Higher dose (2mg) restores more aggressively. Connective tissue repair benefits directly complement knee and QL recovery. Also solves the waste problem: 25 days/vial fits within 28-day shelf life = zero waste.

### Injection Note
BPC-157/TB-500 may be injected subcutaneously near the right knee or lower back/QL region to enhance local tissue repair signaling, rather than only abdominally.

### Known Waste
Zero (at current dosing and smart vial opening rules)

### BAC Water
55ml total → 2 × 30ml vials
