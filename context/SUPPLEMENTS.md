# Supplement Stack

Both subjects cycle (road cycling), weight lift, and swim. Supplements are chosen to support the peptide regimen AND athletic performance/recovery.

## Tier 1 — Essential (Start Day One)

### Creatine Monohydrate
- **Dose:** 5g/day
- **Timing:** Any time, with water
- **Why:** ATP regeneration for cycling power output, lifting strength, and swim sprints. Also supports cognitive function and recovery between sessions. Most studied supplement in existence. No loading phase needed — just 5g daily.

### Vitamin C
- **Dose:** 1,000mg/day
- **Timing:** Morning with food
- **Why:** Essential cofactor for collagen synthesis — directly amplifies GHK-Cu's collagen-building effects. Also provides antioxidant protection during accelerated tissue remodeling.

### Omega-3 Fish Oil
- **Dose:** 2–3g combined EPA/DHA
- **Timing:** Split AM/PM with meals
- **Why:** Anti-inflammatory via COX/LOX pathway — complementary to KPV's NF-κB mechanism. Supports joint health critical for high-volume multi-sport training.

### Vitamin D3 + K2
- **Dose:** 5,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2 (MK-7)
- **Timing:** Morning with fat-containing food
- **Why:** Immune modulation, bone density for impact activities, muscle function. K2 directs calcium to bones (not arteries). Most people are deficient.

### Magnesium Glycinate
- **Dose:** 400mg/day
- **Timing:** Before bed
- **Why:** Muscle recovery from training, sleep quality improvement, cramping prevention (relevant for cycling). Glycinate form is gut-friendly alongside BPC-157. Also supports lower back/QL issues for Subject B.

## Tier 2 — Recommended (Add If Budget Allows)

### Collagen Peptides
- **Dose:** 10–15g/day
- **Timing:** Morning in coffee or smoothie
- **Why:** Provides raw amino acid building blocks (glycine, proline, hydroxyproline) that GHK-Cu needs to synthesize new collagen in tendons, skin, and connective tissue.

### Zinc
- **Dose:** 25–30mg/day
- **Timing:** With food (avoid taking simultaneously with copper supplements)
- **Why:** Wound healing, immune support, testosterone maintenance during heavy training loads.

### NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine)
- **Dose:** 600mg 2×/day
- **Timing:** Empty stomach, AM and PM
- **Why:** Glutathione precursor — antioxidant defense during accelerated tissue remodeling and heavy training adaptation. Supports liver function.

### Probiotics (Multi-strain)
- **Dose:** 50+ billion CFU
- **Timing:** Morning, empty stomach
- **Why:** Gut microbiome support. Complements BPC-157 and KPV's gut-healing effects by repopulating beneficial bacteria.

## Tier 3 — Optional (Nice to Have)

### Glycine
- **Dose:** 3–5g/day
- **Timing:** Evening (can combine with magnesium)
- **Why:** Additional collagen precursor, sleep quality support, and its own anti-inflammatory properties. Inexpensive and effective.

## Daily Timing Schedule

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| Morning (empty stomach) | Peptide injections (all 3) → Probiotics → wait 20 min |
| Morning (with breakfast) | Creatine · Vitamin D3+K2 · Vitamin C · Omega-3 (half dose) · Collagen peptides |
| Midday (with food) | NAC (1st dose) · Zinc |
| Evening (with dinner) | Omega-3 (half dose) · NAC (2nd dose) |
| Before bed | Magnesium Glycinate · Glycine |

## Training Timing Note

Inject peptides in the morning before training, or at least 30 minutes before a workout. BPC-157/TB-500 may also enhance recovery when injected post-workout. Creatine timing is irrelevant — just take it daily. On heavy training days (long rides, heavy lifts), the recovery benefits of this stack will be most noticeable.
