# The Degradation Argument: Why KLOW Blend Is Problematic

## The Product in Question

**KLOW Blend 80mg** (10 vials/kit) — $458
- GHK-Cu 50mg / BPC-157 10mg / TB-500 10mg / KPV 10mg per vial
- All four peptides combined in a single lyophilized vial

## The Core Problem: Copper-Catalyzed Oxidation

GHK-Cu carries a bound Cu²⁺ ion. Copper is a **redox-active transition metal** that readily catalyzes oxidative degradation of peptides — particularly at methionine, cysteine, histidine, and tryptophan residues.

Once GHK-Cu is in solution with BPC-157 and TB-500, the copper acts as a catalyst for degradation.

## Specific Vulnerabilities

### BPC-157
- Contains a **methionine residue** highly susceptible to copper-catalyzed oxidation
- Methionine → methionine sulfoxide (well-documented degradation pathway)
- Methionine sulfoxide variants are **different molecules** with altered or unknown bioactivity
- This isn't "less peptide" — it's creating new, unintended compounds

### TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)
- Contains **methionine at position 6 (Met⁶)**
- Same oxidative vulnerability as BPC-157
- Larger, more complex peptide that's already somewhat fragile in solution

### KPV
- Lysine, Proline, Valine — **none** are oxidation-sensitive
- Relatively hardy, but sitting in a destabilized chemical environment
- This is why GHK-Cu + KPV works fine as a standalone blend

## Additional Concerns

1. **Different optimal pH ranges** — no single pH keeps all four stable
2. **Peptide-peptide interactions** and aggregation risk increases with complexity
3. **GHK-Cu dominance** (62.5% of blend) means high copper concentration relative to other peptides
4. **Degradation is progressive** — accelerates over time and with temperature/light exposure
5. **Unpredictable** — Vial #1 might be okay; Vial #10 sitting for weeks could be substantially degraded

## The Killer Argument

The same company sells:
- **BPC-157 / TB-500 Blend** (5mg/5mg, 10 vials) — $188 ✅ No copper, no problem
- **Beauty Blend GHK-Cu / KPV** (50mg/20mg, 10 vials) — $143 ✅ No oxidation-sensitive residues
- **KLOW Blend** (all 4 combined) — $458 ❌ Copper + methionine in same vial

They already have two chemically sound blends. KLOW jams them together, introducing the degradation problem. And the three separate products cost **$456 vs $458** — $2 cheaper with no degradation risk.

## What We're NOT Saying

- We're not saying the individual peptides don't work
- We're not saying blending is always bad (GHK-Cu + KPV is fine, BPC-157 + TB-500 is fine)
- We ARE saying that putting a copper-carrying peptide in the same vial as methionine-containing peptides is bad chemistry

## Evidence They'd Need to Prove Us Wrong

The company would need to provide **accelerated stability data** showing all four peptides remain intact over time in the reconstituted blend. We'd be surprised if they could.
