# Domain Knowledge: The 4 Peptides

## BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)

**Role in stack:** The project manager of tissue repair.

- Pentadecapeptide (15 amino acids) originally isolated from human gastric juice
- Contains a **methionine residue** — critical for the degradation argument
- Upregulates growth factor receptors (VEGF, EGF, FGF)
- Promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation to damaged areas)
- Protects and heals the gut lining
- Notable interaction with dopamine and serotonin systems → mood stabilization, mental clarity
- Broadly restorative: tendons, ligaments, gut lining, muscle tissue
- Typical research dose: 250–500mcg/day subcutaneous

## TB-500 (Thymosin Beta-4)

**Role in stack:** The first responder.

- 43-amino acid peptide, naturally one of the first released after injury
- Contains **methionine at position 6 (Met⁶)** — vulnerable to copper oxidation
- Reduces inflammation at injury sites
- Promotes cell migration to damaged areas
- Facilitates tissue remodeling and stem cell mobilization
- Works synergistically with BPC-157: TB-500 does early groundwork (clearing debris, reducing swelling), BPC-157 organizes the sustained repair
- Typical research dose: 250–500mcg/day subcutaneous

## GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)

**Role in stack:** The rebuilder.

- Tripeptide (Gly-His-Lys) naturally bound to a copper(II) ion (Cu²⁺)
- **The copper ion is the source of the degradation problem** — it's a redox-active transition metal
- Stimulates collagen I and III synthesis, elastin production
- Regulates metalloproteinase activity (MMP)
- Resets gene expression in older/damaged tissue toward a younger profile — upregulates hundreds of genes associated with youthful tissue, downregulates aging-associated genes
- Increases antioxidant enzyme levels (SOD, glutathione)
- Natural levels decline with age: ~200ng/ml at age 20 → ~80ng/ml by age 60
- Visible effects: skin texture, fine lines, hair thickness, scar remodeling
- Typical research dose: 1–2mg/day subcutaneous
  - 1mg: conservative, appropriate for younger subjects (<30)
  - 2mg: more aggressive restoration for 30+ or those with connective tissue concerns

## KPV

**Role in stack:** The peacekeeper.

- Tripeptide fragment (Lys-Pro-Val) of alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone (α-MSH)
- **No oxidation-sensitive residues** — lysine, proline, valine are all robust
- This is why GHK-Cu + KPV is a safe blend (the "Beauty Blend")
- Potent anti-inflammatory via melanocortin receptor system
- Suppresses NF-κB, the master switch for systemic inflammation
- Calms gut inflammation, skin conditions (eczema, rosacea), chronic low-grade inflammation
- Works through a complementary pathway to Omega-3 (COX/LOX vs NF-κB)
- **Does NOT need to cycle** — as a simple tripeptide, receptor desensitization is not a concern
- Typical research dose: 250–500mcg/day subcutaneous (or oral for gut-specific)

## Synergy: How They Work Together

1. **BPC-157 + TB-500** tell the body what to fix (coordinate repair)
2. **GHK-Cu** provides the blueprint and materials to rebuild it better (collagen, elastin, gene expression)
3. **KPV** keeps the immune system from getting in the way (NF-κB suppression)

All four simultaneously = repair, regeneration, and inflammation addressed from every angle.
